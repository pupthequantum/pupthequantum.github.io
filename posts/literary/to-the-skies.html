To the Skies

By Catnip Dealer
Mon, October 19, 2020

The monoplane gave off a low, mumbling sound as I start the engine, which released a bout of smoke. My dad flew crop dusters, and I saw him fly his trusty biplane once a week until, well, his lower body got paralyzed after an accident. I saw how he operated his flying machine – almost like it is an extension of his body.

I should not be nervous. Like, come on, I lived beside crop dusters since forever. But I never flew one; this is the first time that I’ll take to the skies. And unfortunately, the nerves caught up to me, for the second time this day. Doubt started to take over – will this thing even fly? Will it burst into flames mid-air? Will I land this and still be alive at the same time?

There’s one way to find out.

I increased the engine’s output, turning the propeller so fast it became a blur. With my monoplane starting to move, I tested the control surfaces one last time. They work as intended. Picking up speed, I closed my eyes out of fear. I anticipated an explosion, or an engine stall, but those didn’t come. As I start to feel light, panic came. Am I already dead?

Slowly, I opened my eyes.

Holy cow, I’m flying!

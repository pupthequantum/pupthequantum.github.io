From Strings to Space

By Adrian Jasper Bayani
Tue, October 13, 2020

All objects have a definite size
From the puny to the vast lengthwise
But to learn the unfathomable
We must see the scale in every angle

Beginning from the string
A theoretical and unknown thing
Which forms the basic particle’s everything
So that atoms may they bring

Looking outward, are compounds and chains
That form together in groups and formations
For simple life to divide and reproduce
And for inanimate objects to stay fused

Next, would be complex life
From the insect to the human alive
Residing on land, sea, and air
Part of the cycle of nature everywhere

After that, would be mankind’s marvels
Showing glory to all its inhabitants
From its soaring towers
To its humbling mansions

In all that has been said and done
So far, the best has yet to come
From here on out
Nature’s glory will joust

Let’s begin in nature’s peaks
The awe its mountain seeks
The length its rivers reach
And the vastness its waters stretch

But all of this comes from her
The Mother that we all share
The Earth in all of her wonder and greatness
Would still humble in superlativeness

Earth is a speck compared to the ginormous
The names are Saturn, Jupiter, Neptune, and Uranus
Such said bodies are extremely humongous
But these don’t compare to what’s in the center of us

Behold! The Sun, or to be exact, a star
Its size is unmatched to all bodies in system solar
But it’s nothing compared to every other
For there are stars that are bigger and brighter

But when such stars group together
They get a nebula, which is even bigger
And such nebulas live in a galaxy
Which we think for us is glory

Alas! Such a galaxy is tiny
Compared to what they are when in unity
Space, on what it is called
But size, is something unfathomed

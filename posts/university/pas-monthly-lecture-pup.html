Exoplanets: A Philippine Astronomical Society Monthly Lecture

By Ronah Ceniza Rollan
Friday, November 29, 2019

Philippine Astronomical Society (PAS), in partnership with PUP Physics Society, held its November Monthly Lecture at Claro M. Recto Hall, PUP Manila last November 16, 2019 with the theme Exoplanets: The Physics and Biochemistry of Searching for the Next Earth .

Lecturers from PAS, Sir Ron Darell Aves and Sir Leogiver Manosca, delivered insights about the said theme. Sir Aves went back in time and sighted how humanity’s search for exoplanets actually began. After his insightful talk, Sir Manosca brought to light the exploration of exoplanet biosignatures, and other signs of life.

With more than 400 participants coming from different schools, universities and institutions, ranging from high school students to doctoral students and other enthusiasts in the field, this Monthly Lecture holds the record for having the most number of attendees.

We didn’t expect the venue (Claro M. Recto Hall) will reach its full capacity unlike last year although this was still the venue.

Andrian Floro, PUP Physics Society President

This Monthly Lecture is part of the organization’s project called PAS Continuing Education Program (PASCEP), a public outreach program in astronomy and astrophysics. This project aims to propel astronomy education among Filipinos.

The number of participants has been exponentially increasing since its first Monthly Lecture in partnership with the PUP Physics Society. An increase in the number of participants for the next Monthly Lecture is expected.

John William Latuja, a PAS member and now a part of the PUP Physics Society said that it is a great opportunity to be a member of both organizations. He also said that he was amazed, while speaking on the podium, how full-packed the venue was.

Astronomy does not only end in public lectures such as this one furthermore, both PAS and PUP Physics Society conducts Stargazing Camps.
